Month 1

Nesting & training ramp

They’ll cover the training schedule in 8 days. Nesting then runs for 3 weeks, where they gradually build confidence with every channel — email, calls, and chat.

🐦

Months 2–3

Core ticket scope — basics

Stay on simpler case types while nesting and quality routines settle.

Providers Renfe, Movelia, Ouigo, Iryo, Italo, Assertis.

  • Changes and cancellations within standard paths.
  • Baseline complaints — templates, tone, when to escalate.
  • Missed request — work the process and templates through to resolution.
  • Apply coach and QA feedback to build speed and consistency on those basics.
🐛

Months 4–5

Harder complaints + bugs + fraud

Queue mix widens: harder complaints plus new work types.

Providers Eurostar, Benerail, Trenitalia, SNCF.

  • More complex complaints (still with guardrails).
  • Bugs — log cleanly, set expectations, hand off as per process.
  • Fraud — use the playbook, verification steps, and agreed escalation paths.
✈️

Mid-year

Flights — start live handling

Start handling live flight cases in the queue, building on the month-1 flights intro and coach support.

  • Understand the difference between Travelfusion and Travelport.
  • Know when to escalate to the air team and when to resolve without an air escalation.
  • Learn to use Travelfusion reports as part of your workflow.
  • Follow the flight team’s instructions and guidance.
